# UI Design Directions

> **Status:** exploration. Three candidate visual directions for the same dashboard; one will be chosen for the clickable prototype.

The same Tyche Lens dashboard was mocked in three visual directions. Branding uses the name "Tyche Lens" with a violet accent (`#6b4dfb`).

## A · Terminal — dark, dense, pro

A Bloomberg-terminal feel: dark background, a dense top ticker row, large portfolio value, a prominent price chart, and compact watchlist / allocation / news panels. High information density, monospace-leaning numerics. Best for power users who want a lot on screen.

## B · Fintech — light, airy, friendly

White rounded cards on a light surface, generous spacing, the violet accent for emphasis, and a clear hierarchy (a large "total portfolio value" card, chart, and sections). A dark variant also exists. Approachable and modern; lower density than Terminal.

## C · Editorial — calm, typographic

A quieter, reading-first layout: serif headings, lots of whitespace, restrained color. Treats the page more like a well-set document than a control panel. Calmest of the three; lowest density.

## Open question

No direction is chosen yet. The core trade-off is **information density vs. approachability** (A → B → C, densest to calmest).

The original mockups, wireframes, and screenshots came from a Claude Design playground and are **not included in this repository**. The summary above captures what matters for the design decision.
